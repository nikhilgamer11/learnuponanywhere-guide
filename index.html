A — Where and Who
Where exactly do you want learning to appear?
The location determines the iFrame approach, SSO requirements, and what the learner experience will feel like. Be as specific as possible.
Qualify furtherMobile app embedding — app architecture must be confirmed first
Risk
Mobile embedding works well on hybrid apps but is not supported on fully native apps without a Webview. This needs qualifying before you scope anything.
Say this
"Before I scope this, can you tell me how your app is built — is it native Swift or Kotlin, or does it use a wrapper like React Native or Flutter?"
Further discovery to ask
"How is your mobile app built — native (Swift/Kotlin), Pure Hybrid/Wrapper, or Cross-Platform (React Native, Flutter)?"
"Are you planning to use a WebView to serve the learning content inside the app?"
"What percentage of your learners would access training via the mobile app today?"
"Do all learners use the same app, or are there different versions per customer or region?"
Strong signalCRM embedding — high-value use case, lead with the Salesforce demo
Proof
Visa reduced their support team from 5 to 0.5 by embedding training in the customer journey. 1 support ticket per 10,000 logins.
Demo tip
Show the learner completing a course without leaving the CRM view. This is the wow moment that moves deals forward.
Who is the primary audience for this learning?
Audience type determines the proof point to lead with and how to frame the demo.
Strong signalFrontline workforce — classic Anywhere use case
Proof
Chick-fil-A: 300,000 users across 3,000 restaurants, compliance training embedded in their Pathway app. Staff complete training right within the app.
Lead with
"Staff are on the shop floor all day — they never reach a desktop. Anywhere puts training on the device they already have in their pocket."
Qualify furtherInternal L&D — confirm Anywhere is genuinely needed here
Risk
Internal L&D can often be solved with a standard LMS setup. Confirm Anywhere is needed — not just the regular LearnUpon platform.
Ask
"What is the reason training cannot live in the LearnUpon portal directly? Is there a specific workflow or tool you need it to sit inside?"
Do they have a clear MVP goal for what to deliver first?
A focused MVP means the test can be scoped cleanly. Vague scope means the test will be hard to define and even harder to validate.
Action neededNo clear MVP — help them define one before scoping the test
Risk
Without a defined MVP, the test scope will be unclear and the deal will stall. Your job is to help them narrow to one use case.
Say this
"Let us focus on one thing first. If you could embed one course in one place for one audience — what would that be?"
Ask
"What is the single biggest training friction your team faces today? That is usually where we start."
B — Mobile Architecture
How is their platform or mobile app built?
The most important technical question in any Anywhere deal. Get this answer before demoing anything on mobile.
High riskFully native app — Anywhere is not natively supported
Risk
LUA loads into an iFrame (wrapper). Native apps built in Swift or Kotlin do not have a WebView by default. This requires significant dev work and carries high unknowns.
Say this
"LearnUpon Anywhere loads inside a WebView — a browser window within the app. If your app is fully native today, your dev team would need to add WebView support. Is that something they could explore?"
Do not
Do not demo mobile Anywhere or commit to a mobile test until the Webview question is resolved.
Alternative
Offer mobile browser as an interim option — fully supported, no limitations, zero dev work required.
Further discovery to ask
"Is there any plan to add WebView capability to the app architecture in the near term?"
"Who from your dev team would own the Webview implementation, and how long would that realistically take?"
"Would mobile browser be acceptable as a starting point while the WebView work is scoped?"
Proceed with careHybrid app — works well but two known limitations apply
Limitations
Video/audio file downloads are blocked by default. Assignment recording is blocked by mobile permissions. Both are fixable with a short code change from their dev team.
Ask
"Do learners need to download video or audio for offline use? Will you use assignment modules requiring video or audio recording?"
Must askApp architecture not confirmed — ask this first on the call
Ask first
"Before we go further — how is your app built? Native Swift or Kotlin, or a wrapper framework like React Native or Flutter?"
Green signalBrowser-based — fully supported, no limitations
Status
Desktop and mobile browser is fully supported. All module types work. SCORM/xAPI works. White-labelled portals supported. Best starting point for any deal.
Competitor
LUA is the most lightweight and easiest to implement headless LMS — more so than Absorb or Docebo. Neither competitor supports native apps either.
Is mobile-first a critical value driver for this deal?
"Mobile-first" means different things to different prospects. This answer determines how much the app architecture risk matters.
High riskMobile-critical — validate app architecture before progressing
Risk
If mobile is the core requirement and they have a native app, this deal has significant implementation risk. Do not progress to demo without confirming the app architecture.
Ask
"When you say mobile-first — is mobile the only access method, or would desktop browser be acceptable as a starting point during the initial test?"
C — Content and Media
What content types are they planning to use in Anywhere?
Some content types have known limitations in embedded environments — particularly on mobile. Select the most complex type they need.
Action requiredDispatched SCORM — Content Vault must be enabled before the test
Risk
SCORM dispatched from an external tool (Articulate, Adobe Captivate etc.) needs Content Vault enabled in the LU portal settings. Missing this step breaks content in the embedded view.
Action
Confirm Content Vault is enabled before scoping the test. Quick fix but cannot be skipped.
Ask
"Is your SCORM content dispatched from Articulate or another platform, or is it uploaded directly into LearnUpon?"
Technical flagAssignment recording blocked on mobile by default
Risk
Video and audio recording in Assignment modules is blocked by default on hybrid and native mobile apps due to iFrame permission restrictions.
Fix
Fixable with a short iFrame permission code change by their dev team. Flag before scoping the test.
Ask
"Is assignment recording critical to your first use case, or could it be excluded from the initial test scope?"
Not supportedOffline download not available on hybrid or native mobile apps
Risk
Video and audio file downloads are blocked on hybrid and native mobile apps. Content can be streamed but not downloaded for offline use.
Say this
"Offline download is not supported in the embedded experience on mobile apps. Streaming works fine — but if offline download is a hard requirement, we need to discuss alternatives."
Ask
"Is offline download a hard requirement or a nice-to-have? Most learners with mobile connectivity find streaming completely acceptable."
Are they embedding full courses or specific modules only?
LUA is built for full learner experience — it supports full courses. Module-only embedding is not supported.
LimitationModule-only embedding is not supported in LUA
Risk
LearnUpon Anywhere supports full courses only. Individual module-only embedding is not available.
Say this
"LUA embeds at the course level. If you need a single standalone module, we would wrap it in a short course first — which is a quick admin step."
Ask
"Would wrapping the content in a short course work for your use case, or is standalone module access a hard requirement?"
D — Technical Setup
Do they have Single Sign-On (SSO) configured?
SSO determines how seamless the learner experience will be. SAML and SQSSO are both supported by LearnUpon.
BlockerNo SSO — test setup will be significantly more complex
Risk
Without SSO, learners cannot access the embedded experience seamlessly. The test can still run but setup is more complex and the learner experience will have friction.
Introduce
SQSSO is the simplest option — no full IDP required. Can be set up quickly. Worth presenting this as the path of least resistance.
Ask
"Is SSO on your roadmap? Even a basic SQSSO implementation would significantly simplify both the test setup and the final learner experience."
Further discovery to ask
"How do your users currently log in to the platform where learning will appear?"
"Is there a plan to implement SSO in the near term? Which IDP would you use?"
"Would SQSSO — LearnUpon's own simpler SSO option — be acceptable as a starting point?"
ValidateSSO exists but needs validation against LearnUpon
Action
Schedule a 30-minute technical call with their IT contact to validate the SSO connection before the test starts.
Ask
"Which IDP are you using — Okta, Azure AD, Google? Is it SAML-based?"
Technical checkCookieless auth — IDP compatibility must be confirmed
Risk
SAML + Cookieless can break depending on the IDP provider. Test compatibility as part of the technical validation call before scoping.
Do they have development resource available?
No dev resource means Grab & Go is the only viable option. Scoping a Custom iFrame without a developer is the most common mistake in Anywhere deals.
Scope changeNo dev resource — redirect to Grab & Go immediately
Action
Do not scope a Custom iFrame deal. Grab & Go is the only realistic option — LearnUpon provides a code snippet, no development required.
Say this
"If you do not have dev resource right now, we have a Grab & Go option — LearnUpon provides a code snippet you paste into any page. Fastest way to get started with zero engineering involvement."
Qualify furtherLimited dev resource — get a named contact before leaving this call
Ask
"Who specifically from your technical team would own the integration side — even part-time? I need a name before we set a test start date."
Ask
"Can we get two to three days of their time in the next two weeks for the initial setup?"
Must confirmTechnical contact not identified — get a name on this call
Ask
"Who from your technical team would own the integration side of the test? I need to send them the Getting Started documentation directly."
E — Experience and Readiness
How do users currently authenticate into the platform where Anywhere will appear?
Authentication method determines the SSO approach and iFrame configuration needed for the test.
Setup neededNo SSO — SQSSO setup required before test can begin
Action
Introduce SQSSO as a quick, low-friction path. Does not require a full IDP setup and can be implemented quickly.
Ask
"Does your team have the ability to generate a token server-side? SQSSO is a short implementation that would get authentication working quickly."
Has a technical contact been identified on their side?
Without a named technical owner, the test will stall the moment it reaches the integration phase. This is an SE blocker — not an AE one.
Ready to proceedTechnical contact confirmed — send Getting Started docs today
Do this
Send the Getting Started documentation directly to the named technical contact today. Do not let this wait — the test starts from the moment they receive it.
Say this
"I will send the Getting Started guide directly to [name] today. It covers API authentication, SSO configuration, and the iFrame code examples. Typical setup time is one to two days."
Get a nameTeam identified but no owner — get a specific name before you hang up
Risk
Sending Getting Started docs to a team inbox means no one owns it. The test stalls with no one accountable.
Ask
"Who specifically within the team would own this — even part-time? I need a direct contact to send the technical documentation to."
BlockerNo technical contact — test cannot be scoped without one
Risk
This is a hard blocker for the test. The integration requires someone who can configure SSO, set CSP headers, and build or configure the iFrame. Without a named owner, the deal will not progress past this point.
Say this
"To move forward with a test, I need a technical resource on your side — even a few days of their time. Who in your organisation manages your platform integrations or API connections?"
Ask
"Is there an IT team, a CTO, or an external agency who owns your platform technically? They would be my contact for the Getting Started documentation."
Confirm capacityExternal agency — confirm availability and access before scoping the test
Risk
External agencies add a dependency on their availability and their access to the prospect's platform. This can significantly extend the test timeline.
Ask
"Does the agency have access to your platform environment? And do they have availability in the next two to three weeks to work on this?"
Ask
"Is there a direct technical contact at the agency I can send the Getting Started documentation to?"
